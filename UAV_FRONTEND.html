1.MAIN WING SIZING CONSTRASINTS

The main wing generates the lift necessary to overcome weight

Wing Loading: Lower wing loading less than 30 gives slower stall speeds which are good for gliders/trainer planes. Higher loading speeds up cruise but requires longer runaway.

Aspect Ratio (AR = b² / S): Slenderness of the wing. Higher AR minimizes induced drag (wingtip vortices), increasing efficiency at the expense of structural stiffness.

2.TAIL VOLUME COEFF AND ASPECT RATIOS

Tail volume coefficients : are dimensionless values used by aerospace engineers to guarantee pitch (longitudinal) and yaw (directional) stability:

Horizontal Tail Coefficient (Vh): Vh = (Sh * Lt) / (S * MAC) Recommended range: 0.35 to 0.65. Provides sufficient counter-acting pitching moment to stabilize the wing and maintain pitch trim.

Horizontal Tail Coefficient (Vh): Vh = (Sh * Lt) / (S * MAC) Recommended range: 0.35 to 0.65. Provides sufficient counter-acting pitching moment to stabilize the wing and maintain pitch trim.

Horizontal Tail Aspect Ratio: It provide primary stablization for pitch stablity.Optimizes longitudinal pitch stability. Higher values yield a slender, highly efficient tail plane that delivers rapid pitch corrections with minimal drag, but induces severe root bending stress requiring structural mass tradeoffs.

Vertical Tail Coeff

Controls directional yaw damping. Increasing this parameter creates a taller stabilizer that aggressively counters side-slip and heading deviations, though it escalates torsional twisting loads on the rear fuselage and elevates sensitivity to crosswind gusts during critical landing phases.

ENVIROMENT AND BALANCE

Max Lift Coeff: Defines the aerodynamic limit of the airfoil. Increasing this value directly reduces the UAV's stall speed, allowing it to fly slower and take off from shorter runways, but typically requires high-lift devices or specialized camber airfoils that can introduce structural profiling complexity

Angle Of Attack: The angle between the wing's chord line and the oncoming airflow. Sliding this higher scales up the instantaneous lift generation for climbing, but pushing it past the critical limit disrupts smooth airflow over the upper surface, triggering sudden boundary layer separation and catastrophic aerodynamic stall.

Payload Balance shift: Simulates internal mass movement relative to the mean aerodynamic center. Shifting this slider alters the Longitudinal Center of Gravity (CG); moving it forward demands constant tail trimming drag to counter a heavy nose, while moving it too far aft diminishes pitch restoring stability, making flight completely uncontrollable.
